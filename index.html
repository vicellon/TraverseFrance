Top 20 things to do in france
France, the most memorable country to visit. Located in Western Europe, the land of romance, sharing borders with Belgium, Luxembourg, Germany, Switzerland, Italy and Spain.
Experience the Eiffel Tower, The Louvre Museum, and the Palace of Versailles, A truely breathtaking country. France is also famous for its cuisine which includes dishes like croissants and Escargot, sails cooked in butter sauce with garlic.
Discover the best, and make the most of your visit with the top 20 rated things to do in France.
1. Visit The Eiffel Tower
Eiffel Tower at sunset
To experience Paris at it’s most enchanting, visit the Eiffel Tower during the sunset. The way the early evening lights glow and illuminate the buildings below almost puts a spell over the city, giving it the most romantic setting.
The higher you go, the more spectacular the city appears as the sun goes down. The way the city’s monuments and the Seine River reflect the golden rays of the sun will give you goosebumps. And as an extra touch, the Eiffel Tower is fitted with glittering lights that sparkle for a few minutes every hour.
A truly memory drilling experience is to watch the sunset as you dine from one of the restaurant on the Eiffel Tower: Madame Brasserie, a comtemporary brasserie on the first level that offers a seasonal menu, or the Le Jules gastronomic restaurant on the second level that serves Michelin-Star quality French cuisine.
The view from both restaurant’s dining rooms feature jaw-dropping views of the cityscape of Paris. It is recommended that you request a window seat.
2. Experience The Louvre Museum
The Louvre Museum
Even the main entrance way to the Musee de Louvre is an artistic dream, as is befitting to the world's most visited museum. But there's not much time to linger gazing up at the glassy pyramid.
For inside awaits the fantasy of the most ethereal collection of art the world has seen, with everything from the Code of Hammurabi, to Egyptian mummies, to Leonardo's Mona Lisa, and the Venus de Milo.
The museum has endured the centuries-old political and social transformations of France, while still maintaining the vastness and beauty of the human mind under a single roof, and thereby becoming a potent symbolization of French finesse and sensitivity.
